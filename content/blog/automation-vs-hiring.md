Do not automate a process you do not understand. First make the workflow visible: inputs, decisions, exceptions, owners, and failure points.

Hire when judgment is the bottleneck. Automate when repetition is the bottleneck. The best systems usually do both: people handle decisions, software handles movement, reminders, sync, reporting, and validation.

Start with the smallest reliable automation that removes daily friction. If it saves time every week and reduces mistakes, it has earned its place.
